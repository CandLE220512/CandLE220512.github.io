6. CandLE promptly disposes of personal information once the purpose of processing has been achieved. The process, timeline, and method of disposal are as follows.

Process for Disposal
Information provided by users is transferred to a separate database (or stored in a separate document in the case of paper records) after the purpose has been achieved. It is then either retained for a certain period or immediately disposed of in accordance with internal policies and relevant laws. In this case, personal information transferred to the database will not be used for any other purpose unless required by law.

- Deadline for Disposal
When the retention period of the user’s personal information has expired, it will be disposed of within five days from the end date of the retention period. If the personal information is no longer needed due to the fulfillment of its processing purpose, termination of the relevant service, or closure of the business, it will be disposed of within five days from the date it is determined to be no longer necessary.

- Method of Disposal
Personal information in electronic file format will be disposed of using a technical method that makes the records unrecoverable.
Personal information printed on paper will be disposed of by shredding or incineration.

9. Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Information
In accordance with Article 29 of the Personal Information Protection Act, CandLE implements the following technical, administrative, and physical measures to ensure the security of personal information.

① Regular Internal Audits
CandLE conducts regular internal audits on a quarterly basis to ensure the security of personal information management.

② Minimization and Training of Personnel Handling Personal Information
CandLE designates specific personnel to handle personal information and implements measures to limit management to authorized personnel only, minimizing access.

③ Establishment and Implementation of an Internal Management Plan
CandLE has established and is implementing an internal management plan to ensure the secure processing of personal information.

④ Encryption of Personal Information
Users’ personal information, including passwords, is stored and managed in encrypted form, ensuring that only the user can access it. For sensitive data, additional security features, such as encrypting files and transmission data or applying file locks, are implemented.
